Uncovering CA School District Debt

Between 2002 and 2018, school district debt in the United States surged by 112%, forcing cuts to critical resources like teachers and classrooms.
This platform empowers users to explore a first-of-its-kind dataset combining U.S. Census and California State Controller data to analyze the
causes and trends behind school district debt. Through interactive tools and accessible downloads, we aim to increase transparency
and provide insights to drive meaningful change.

Growing Burden of Debt

School district debt in the U.S. surged by 112% from 2002 to 2018, with 10-20% of budgets now spent on repayments, impacting classrooms and teachers.

Comprehensive Data Analysis

This project analyzes 938 California school districts, combining U.S. Census and California State Controller data to uncover trends in borrowing, expenditures, and local property values.

Insights Through Time

With data spanning over a decade since 2008, our tools provide unprecedented insights to identify the causes and patterns of growing school district debt.
